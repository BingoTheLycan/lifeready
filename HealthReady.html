🧠 MENTAL HEALTH GUIDE
MENTAL HEALTH & SELF-ADVOCACY

Mental health is health. These are real skills for managing stress, recognizing when to ask for help, and advocating for yourself.

💜 A note: This guide covers mental health basics for learning purposes. If you or someone you know is struggling right now, please reach out to someone you trust - or call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, free, 24/7).
💜
YOU HAVE THE RIGHT TO SPEAK UP
Self-advocacy means being an active participant in your own care. Ask questions when you do not understand. Describe your symptoms clearly. Ask for accommodations. You have the right to be heard.
🔍
SIGNS YOU MAY NEED SUPPORT
Talk to a doctor or counselor if you have: persistent sadness or hopelessness for 2+ weeks · anxiety that interferes with daily life · thoughts of hurting yourself · changes in sleep or appetite that do not improve
💪
HEALTHY STRESS MANAGEMENT
Evidence-based strategies: physical activity · deep breathing (inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4) · connecting with supportive people · doing something you enjoy · limiting caffeine and alcohol · getting consistent sleep
🆘
CRISIS SUPPORT
988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline - call or text 988, available 24/7 · Crisis Text Line - text HOME to 741741 · If someone is in immediate danger, call 911 · Never keep thoughts of self-harm a secret - getting help is the right thing to do
😴
SLEEP & MENTAL HEALTH
7-9 hours per night for most adults · Consistent sleep and wake times help regulate mood · Poor sleep worsens anxiety and depression · If sleep problems persist, talk to your doctor - sleep disorders are treatable
💬
DESCRIBING MENTAL HEALTH SYMPTOMS
Be specific with your doctor: what you feel · where in your body · how long it has been happening · how it affects your daily life · severity on a 1-10 scale. Example: "I have felt very anxious every morning for 2 weeks. It is making it hard to go to work. About a 7 out of 10."
📞 HEALTH CALL SCRIPTS
WHAT TO SAY
Real scripts for real health situations. Practice these out loud so the words feel natural when you need them.
📅
MAKING A DOCTOR APPOINTMENT
"Hi, my name is [name]. I would like to make an appointment. I am a [new/existing] patient. I have been having [symptom] for about [time]. Do you have anything available this week?"
💬
DESCRIBING SYMPTOMS CLEARLY
"I have been having [type of pain] in my [location] for [how long]. On a scale of 1-10 it is about a [number]. It gets worse when I [activity] and better when I [activity]."
🙋
ASKING FOR CLARIFICATION
"I am sorry - I did not quite understand that. Could you explain it in simpler terms?"

"Could you write that down for me?"
📆
CANCELING OR RESCHEDULING
"Hi, I have an appointment scheduled for [date/time] and need to cancel or reschedule. I am sorry for the short notice. What is the earliest available time to rebook?"
☎️
CALLING POISON CONTROL - 1-800-222-1222
"My name is [name]. I believe someone swallowed [substance]. They are [age] years old and weigh about [weight]. They are [conscious/unconscious]. What should I do?"
♿
REQUESTING AN ACCOMMODATION
"I have a disability and will need some accommodations for my appointment. I need [specific accommodation]. Can you note that in my file so the office is prepared?"
💊
ASKING ABOUT MEDICATION COST
"I was just prescribed this medication and I am concerned about the cost. Is there a generic version available? Are there any discount programs or coupons I can use?"

💊 PRESCRIPTIONS GUIDE
READING YOUR PRESCRIPTION LABEL

Your prescription label contains everything you need to take your medication safely. Here is what each part means.

👤
PATIENT NAME
Always check that the name on the label is yours before taking any medication. Never take someone else's prescription.
📋
DOSE INSTRUCTIONS
This tells you exactly how much to take, when to take it, and how. Examples: Take 1 tablet twice daily with food. Take as needed for pain.
🔄
REFILLS
Refills: 2 means you can pick up this prescription 2 more times without a new doctor visit. Refills: 0 means you need a new prescription.
📅
EXPIRATION DATE
Never take expired medication. It may have lost effectiveness or become unsafe. Ask your pharmacist about proper disposal.
💡
COMMON ABBREVIATIONS
PRN = as needed · QD = once daily · BID = twice daily · TID = three times daily · QID = four times daily · AC = before meals · PC = after meals
😴
MAY CAUSE DROWSINESS
Check with your pharmacist before driving or operating machinery. You may need to take this at night or adjust the timing.
🚫
DO NOT DRINK ALCOHOL
Some medications interact dangerously with alcohol - even in small amounts. Follow this warning exactly.
✅
TAKE UNTIL GONE
Always finish all medication in the bottle, even if you feel better. Stopping early can allow the infection to return - sometimes stronger.
❄️
KEEP REFRIGERATED
Storage instructions are not optional. Move it to the fridge immediately. Ask your pharmacist if it has been stored wrong.
🤔
QUESTIONS TO ASK
Your pharmacist is free to talk to and knows your medications. Ask: Is it safe to drive? Can I take this with my other medications? What side effects should I watch for?
💰
IF YOU CANNOT AFFORD IT
Ask your pharmacist about: generic alternatives · GoodRx or similar discount apps · Manufacturer patient assistance programs · Asking your doctor for samples or a lower-cost alternative
👍
ALWAYS SAY YES TO COUNSELING
When a pharmacist asks if you want counseling, always say yes. It is free and they will explain exactly how to take your medication safely.
🩺 DOCTOR VISITS GUIDE
MAKING THE MOST OF YOUR APPOINTMENT

Being prepared makes every doctor visit more effective. Here is what to do before, during, and after.

📝
BEFORE: PREPARE
Write down: your symptoms (what, where, when it started, how severe) · your questions · all medications, vitamins, and supplements you take · your insurance card and ID
⏰
ARRIVE EARLY
Plan to arrive 10-15 minutes before your appointment time to check in, complete forms, and be ready. Arriving late can mean a shortened or rescheduled appointment.
💬
DURING: BE SPECIFIC
Describe symptoms specifically: location · type of sensation (sharp, dull, burning, aching) · when it started · how often · what makes it better or worse · severity on a scale of 1-10
🙋
ASK QUESTIONS
You have the right to understand your own health. Say: I do not understand - can you explain that differently? Can you write that down? What does that mean for my daily life?
📅
AFTER: FOLLOW THROUGH
Before you leave: schedule follow-up appointments · get your prescriptions · write down your diagnosis and instructions · ask the front desk any billing questions
🪪 HEALTH INSURANCE GUIDE
UNDERSTANDING YOUR HEALTH INSURANCE

Health insurance can feel confusing. These are the key terms and concepts - plain and simple.

🪪
YOUR INSURANCE CARD
Your card has your Member ID - your personal insurance number. You need this at every appointment. The front shows your name, Member ID, group number, and plan name. The back has phone numbers you can call with questions about your coverage.
🩺
PRIMARY CARE PHYSICIAN (PCP)
Your PCP is your main doctor - the one you go to for checkups and when you feel sick. Some insurance plans require you to see your PCP first before you can see a specialist. Think of your PCP as your home base for health care.
💰
COPAY
A copay is a fixed amount you pay each visit or prescription. For example - $20 every time you see your doctor, and insurance pays the rest. Your copay amount is printed on your insurance card.
🗺️
IN-NETWORK vs OUT-OF-NETWORK
In-network: The doctor or hospital has an agreement with your insurance. You pay less.

Out-of-network: No agreement. You pay more - sometimes a lot more. Always check if a provider is in-network before making an appointment.
📊
DEDUCTIBLE
Your deductible is how much you pay on your own before insurance kicks in. Example: if your deductible is $500, you pay the first $500 of bills yourself each year. After that, insurance starts helping. Preventive care like annual checkups is usually covered before you hit your deductible.
📅
OPEN ENROLLMENT
Open enrollment is the one time each year you can sign up for or change your health insurance. If you miss it, you usually have to wait until next year - unless you have a major life change like losing a job, getting married, or having a baby. Mark your calendar.
📋
PRIOR AUTHORIZATION
Prior authorization means your insurance has to approve something before you get it - a test, surgery, or medication. Your doctor usually requests this. If you skip prior auth when it is required, insurance may refuse to pay. Ask your doctor if anything needs prior authorization before your appointment.
📄
EXPLANATION OF BENEFITS (EOB)
An EOB is a document your insurance sends after a medical visit. It shows what was billed, what insurance paid, and what you owe. It is not a bill. Compare your EOB to any bill you receive - billing errors are common, and you have the right to dispute them.
🐍
COBRA
COBRA lets you keep your work health insurance for up to 18 months after losing your job. The catch - you pay the full cost yourself, which can be expensive. It is a safety net while you look for new coverage. Your employer must notify you about COBRA within 14 days of losing your job.
❓
GOT A CONFUSING BILL?
1. Request an itemized bill - a list of every charge
2. Compare it to your EOB from your insurance company
3. Call the billing department if something does not match
4. You can dispute errors - do not just pay without understanding the bill
🚨 EMERGENCY GUIDE
WHEN AND HOW TO GET HELP

Knowing when to call 911, go to urgent care, or wait can save lives and unnecessary stress.

🚨
CALL 911 FOR
Not breathing · Chest pain or heart attack symptoms · Stroke (FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty) · Seizure lasting over 5 minutes · Severe bleeding · Suspected poisoning · Unconscious person
🏥
GO TO URGENT CARE FOR
Ear infections · Strep throat · UTIs · Minor cuts that may need stitches (bleeding stopped) · Mild sprains · Rashes · Flu symptoms · Minor burns
⚡
STROKE: THINK FAST
F - Face drooping on one side · A - Arm weakness, one arm drifts down · S - Speech slurred or strange · T - Time to call 911. Every minute matters with a stroke.
☎️
POISON CONTROL
Call Poison Control FIRST for any poisoning: 1-800-222-1222. Available 24/7. They will tell you exactly what to do. Never make someone vomit without being instructed to by Poison Control.
🔥
BURNS
Cool running water for 10-20 minutes. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste - these trap heat and increase infection risk. See a doctor if larger than 3 inches or on face, hands, or joints.
🧼 HYGIENE & WELLNESS GUIDE
DAILY HEALTH HABITS

Small daily habits have a big impact on your long-term health, your confidence, and how others interact with you.

🖐️
HANDWASHING
Wash with soap for 20 seconds (long enough to sing Happy Birthday twice). Key times: before eating · after using the bathroom · after sneezing or coughing · after touching shared surfaces
🦷
DENTAL CARE
Brush twice a day for 2 minutes (morning and before bed) · Floss daily · See a dentist every 6 months · Regular cleanings prevent painful and expensive emergencies
🚿
BATHING & CLOTHING
Shower or bathe every day or every other day · Change socks and underwear daily - wearing them again traps bacteria and can cause skin infections and odor
😴
SLEEP
Most adults need 7-9 hours per night. Consistent sleep supports immune function, mental health, and decision-making. Chronic sleep deprivation increases risk of serious health problems.
🤒
WHEN YOU ARE SICK
Stay home until: fever-free for 24-48 hours without fever-reducing medication AND symptoms clearly improving. Cough or sneeze into your elbow or a tissue, not your hands.

🩷 THE STORY

HealthReady was built as the eighth piece of the LifeReady suite. Health literacy is one of the most important - and most overlooked - life skills there is. Reading a prescription label, knowing when to call 911, understanding your insurance card, and being able to describe your symptoms clearly: these things affect your safety and independence every single day.

This app was built because health literacy training does not always happen - especially for people with disabilities, people in transition programs, and people who learned differently in school. HealthReady is here to change that - free, available anytime, built with the same care as every other app in the suite.

🚀 THE MISSION

Health literacy should not be a privilege. HealthReady helps people practice the real skills of health independence - reading prescriptions, navigating the healthcare system, knowing when to call 911, understanding insurance, and advocating for themselves. Free, accessible, and built for everyone.

🤝 A DEDICATION

For the job coaches, support workers, nurses, and special educators who know that health independence is real independence. And for everyone who has ever left a doctor's office confused, been handed a prescription they could not read, or faced a health situation and had no idea what to do. You can. You will.

If any of the topics in this app bring up something you are going through personally - please talk to someone. Call or text 988 any time. You do not have to figure it out alone.
