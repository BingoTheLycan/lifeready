📖 JOB GUIDE
🎤 INTERVIEW TIPS
💡
Always say thank you at the end. It shows you are polite and grateful.
💡
When asked why you want the job, mention something specific about the role you actually like.
💡
It is okay to pause and think before answering. That shows you are thoughtful, not unprepared.
💡
Dress neatly and arrive a few minutes early. First impressions matter a lot.
🏢 WORKPLACE BEHAVIOR
💡
If you do not understand something, always ask. It is much better to ask than to make a mistake.
💡
When something goes wrong, stay calm, be honest, and offer to fix it. Everyone makes mistakes.
💡
Treat everyone - customers, coworkers, managers - with respect. Every interaction matters.
💵 PAYCHECK BASICS
💡
Gross pay = total earned. Net pay = what you actually take home after deductions.
💡
Deductions include federal tax, state tax, Social Security, and sometimes health insurance.
💡
To find your weekly pay: multiply your hourly rate × hours worked. Example: $12 × 20 = $240.
⏰ PUNCTUALITY TIPS
💡
Plan to arrive 5-10 minutes early. If something unexpected happens, you still have time.
💡
If you will be late, call your supervisor as soon as you know. Never wait until the last minute.
💡
Use TimeReady to practice reading your schedule and calculating travel time!
⚖️ RIGHTS AT WORK
⚖️ YOUR BASIC RIGHTS
✅
You have the right to a safe workplace. If something is dangerous, you can report it without being fired for reporting it.
✅
You have the right to be paid at least minimum wage for every hour you work. Your employer must pay you on time.
✅
You have the right to breaks. Most states require a meal break for shifts over a certain length. Ask your employer or HR about your state's rules.
✅
You cannot be fired or treated unfairly because of your disability, race, gender, religion, or national origin. This is called discrimination and it is illegal.
✅
If you have a disability, you may be entitled to reasonable accommodations - changes to your job or workplace that help you do your work. Ask HR about the ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act).
🚫 WHAT IS HARASSMENT?
🚫
Harassment means someone is treating you in a way that is unwelcome, offensive, or threatening - repeatedly or seriously enough to make your workplace uncomfortable.
🚫
Examples: someone making rude jokes about your disability, calling you names, touching you without permission, or threatening your job if you do not do something you are uncomfortable with.
✅
If this happens to you, write down what happened, when, and who was there. Then report it to your supervisor or HR. You have the right to work without harassment.
🆘 WHO TO TALK TO
1
Your supervisor or manager - for most workplace concerns, this is your first step.
2
HR (Human Resources) - if your concern involves your supervisor, or involves discrimination or harassment.
3
Your job coach or support person - they can help you figure out what to say and go with you if needed.
4
The EEOC (Equal Employment Opportunity Commission) - if you believe you were discriminated against. Visit eeoc.gov or call 1-800-669-4000.
💡 REMEMBER

You are allowed to ask questions about your pay, your hours, and your rights. You are allowed to say no to things that make you uncomfortable. A good employer will respect that.

💛 THE STORY

JobReady was built to answer one question: what happens after you know how to make change and read a clock?

I am an adult with a developmental disability. I built CashReady to teach money skills and TimeReady to teach time skills. But I kept thinking about the bigger picture - the actual job. The interview. The first day. The paycheck you do not understand.

JobReady is where CashReady and TimeReady lead. It is the third piece of the puzzle - and maybe the most important one.

🚀 THE MISSION

Getting a job is hard for everyone. For people with disabilities, it is harder - not because of ability, but because the right preparation tools haven't been there. JobReady exists to change that.

🤝 A DEDICATION

Dedicated to every job coach, support worker, and educator who believes in their clients before their clients believe in themselves. And to the educators, coaches, and support workers who stay late, show up early, and believe before anyone else does - your jobs are safe. This app just does the homework.

🆓 ALWAYS FREE
🆓

JobReady is completely free. No ads, no subscriptions, no paywalls. Built to help people - and that does not have a price tag.
