👁️ SITUATIONS GUIDE
👁️ READING SOCIAL SITUATIONS
1
Pay attention to body language. Crossed arms, avoiding eye contact, short answers, and flat tone often say more than words.
2
Read the energy of the room. Is the conversation light and relaxed, or tense and serious? Matching the energy shows awareness.
3
Watch for discomfort signals. Someone pulling away, giving short answers, or checking their phone may need space.
4
Trust your instincts. If something feels off, it often is. You do not need to be certain to take steps to protect your comfort.
5
Check in rather than assume. "Are you okay?" is always better than guessing.
🚩 SOCIAL RED FLAGS
🚩
Someone pressures you to do something you are uncomfortable with - even subtly.
🚩
You feel like you have to change who you are to be accepted.
🚩
Someone dismisses your feelings or makes you feel bad for having them.
🚩
A relationship makes you feel consistently anxious, guilty, or drained.
💛 FEELINGS GUIDE
💛 NAMING YOUR FEELINGS
😤
Frustrated - Something is not going the way you want. You feel blocked or annoyed.
😔
Sad - Something has hurt you, disappointed you, or you have lost something.
😰
Anxious/Nervous - You feel worried or unsure about something coming up.
😠
Angry - Something feels unfair, disrespectful, or has crossed a line for you.
😶
Overwhelmed - Too much is happening at once and it is hard to process.
😕
Confused - You are not sure what happened, what someone meant, or what to do next.
💪 HEALTHY WAYS TO MANAGE FEELINGS
💡
Name it. Saying "I feel frustrated" helps more than letting the feeling stay unnamed.
💡
Pause before reacting. A few deep breaths before responding can completely change the outcome.
💡
Talk to someone you trust. You do not have to carry difficult feelings alone.
💡
Move your body. A walk, stretching, or any physical activity can help release difficult emotions.
💡
Write about it. Getting feelings onto paper can help you understand them better.
🛡️ BOUNDARIES GUIDE
🛡️ WHAT IS A BOUNDARY?

A boundary is a limit you set about what you are - and are not - comfortable with in relationships and interactions. Boundaries are healthy, normal, and something every person has the right to set.

✅ EXAMPLES OF HEALTHY BOUNDARIES
✅
"I need some time alone - I will talk to you later."
✅
"I am not comfortable lending money."
✅
"Please do not hug me without asking first."
✅
"I do not want to talk about that."
✅
"I am not okay with how you spoke to me just now."
💛 SETTING A BOUNDARY - HOW TO DO IT
1
Be clear and direct. State your boundary simply - no long explanation required.
2
Use "I" language. "I am not comfortable with this" instead of "You always..."
3
Stay calm. You do not need to be angry to set a boundary. A quiet, firm statement works.
4
Hold to it. You do not need to apologize for having a boundary. Hold your ground kindly.
💬 CONVERSATION GUIDE
💬 STARTING A CONVERSATION
💡
Comment on something you share in the moment. "This line is really long, huh?" works perfectly.
💡
Ask an open question. "How was your weekend?" invites more than a yes/no answer.
💡
Give a genuine compliment and follow with a question. "I love your bag - where'd you get it?"
👂 KEEPING A CONVERSATION GOING
👂
Listen actively - make eye contact, nod, and respond to what was actually said.
❓
Ask follow-up questions. Show genuine interest in what they are saying.
⚖️
Balance sharing about yourself with asking about them. Conversations go both ways.
🔄
Build on what they said before sharing your own experience.
👋 ENDING A CONVERSATION
💡
"It was really great talking to you - I need to get going, but take care!"
💡
"I have got to run, but let us do this again sometime."
💡
A warm ending leaves a good impression - always acknowledge the conversation before leaving.
🤝 CONFLICT GUIDE
🤝 STEPS FOR RESOLVING CONFLICT
1
Pause. Take a breath before responding. A reaction in the heat of the moment often makes things worse.
2
Focus on the issue, not the person. "I felt hurt when..." not "You always..."
3
Listen to understand, not just to respond. Let the other person finish before you speak.
4
Look for a compromise. A good resolution means both people feel heard.
5
Check in after. A brief follow-up - "are we okay?" - shows you care about the relationship.
💬 "I" STATEMENTS - HOW THEY WORK
❌
"You always cancel plans at the last minute."
✅
"I feel hurt when plans get cancelled last minute - can we talk about it?"
❌
"You never listen to me."
✅
"I feel like I am not being heard right now - can we slow down?"
📞 CALL SCRIPTS
📞 Not sure what to say? These scripts give you the exact words for tricky social situations. Read them, practice them, make them your own.
🛡️ SETTING A BOUNDARY
IN PERSON OR BY TEXT
"I care about our friendship, but I am not comfortable with [specific thing]. I need you to [specific request]."
You do not need to apologize for having a boundary. State it clearly and warmly.
💬 TELLING SOMEONE THEY HURT YOUR FEELINGS
SCRIPT
"Hey, I wanted to talk to you about something. When [describe what happened], I felt [name your feeling]. I do not think you meant to hurt me, but I wanted you to know."
💡 Keep it about how you felt, not what they did wrong. That keeps the conversation open.
🚫 SAYING NO WHEN PRESSURED
WHEN SOMEONE KEEPS PUSHING
"I already said no, and my answer is not changing. I'd appreciate you respecting that."
You do not owe anyone an explanation for saying no. No is a complete sentence.
🤝 APOLOGIZING SINCERELY
SCRIPT
"I am sorry for [specific thing you did]. I understand that it [describe the impact]. That wasn't okay, and I am going to work on [specific change]."
💡 No "but" after sorry. "I am sorry, but..." cancels the apology.
🆘 TELLING A TRUSTED PERSON SOMETHING IS WRONG
STARTING THE CONVERSATION
"I need to talk to you about something. I am not sure how to say it, but I want you to know that [describe the situation]. I could really use your help."
It is always okay to ask for help. You do not have to have the perfect words. Just start.
💛 SUPPORTING A FRIEND WHO IS STRUGGLING
WHAT TO SAY WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW WHAT TO SAY
"I am here. You do not have to go through this alone. I do not have all the answers, but I am not going anywhere."
💡 Sometimes the most powerful thing you can offer is simply your presence. You do not need to fix anything.

💜 THE STORY

SocialReady was built as the sixth piece of the LifeReady suite. Social skills are some of the most important - and most personal - life skills there are. Reading a room, setting a boundary, working through conflict, knowing what to say and when: these things matter in every relationship, every setting, every day.

This app was built because social skills class does not always happen as often as it should. SocialReady is here to fill the gap - free, available anytime, built with the same care as every other app in the suite.

🚀 THE MISSION

Social connection should not be a mystery. SocialReady helps people practice the real skills behind connection - reading situations, having healthy conversations, setting limits, handling conflict, and understanding their own feelings. Free, accessible, and built for everyone.

🤝 A DEDICATION

For the behaviorists, counselors, social workers, and support staff who show up every day to help people understand themselves and each other. And for everyone who has ever felt out of place in a social situation and wondered if they would ever figure it out. You will.
