🚨 SCAM SPOTTING GUIDE
HOW TO SPOT A SCAM

Scammers are good at what they do. But they almost always leave clues. Here are the most common warning signs.

⏱️
URGENCY
"Act NOW or lose your account!" Scammers create panic so you react before you think. Real companies do not threaten you like this.
🎁
TOO GOOD TO BE TRUE
You won a prize you never entered. You get a huge reward for doing almost nothing. Free money, free laptops, free vacations - all want your information first.
🎴
GIFT CARD PAYMENT
Anyone demanding payment in gift cards is always a scammer. The IRS, courts, utility companies, and police NEVER ask for gift cards. Never.
📋
ASKS FOR PERSONAL INFO
Requests for your SSN, passwords, full credit card numbers, or banking details out of the blue. Real companies already have your info and do not ask this way.
✏️
SPELLING MISTAKES
Scam messages are often written quickly. Look for typos, odd phrasing, or sentences that do not quite make sense for a real company.
🎣
PHISHING
Fake emails or texts that look like they come from your bank, Amazon, PayPal, or the IRS. They link to fake websites that steal your password.
💻
FAKE TECH SUPPORT
Pop-ups or calls claiming your device has a virus and you must call a number. They want remote access to your computer to steal money or install real malware.
💔
ROMANCE SCAM
Someone online builds a friendship or romance, often using a fake military or professional profile, then eventually asks for money or gift cards.
🏆
FAKE LOTTERY / PRIZE
You won something - but you have to pay a "processing fee" or "taxes" first. Real prizes do not work this way. You never pay to receive something you won.
🎭
IMPERSONATION
Someone pretends to be a police officer, the IRS, Social Security, or even a grandchild in trouble. They create fear or urgency to get you to send money fast.
IF YOU THINK YOU WERE SCAMMED
1
Do not panic. Act quickly but calmly. The faster you move, the more you can limit the damage.
2
Change your passwords on any affected accounts immediately.
3
Call your bank using the number on the back of your card if any financial information was shared.
4
Tell a trusted person - a family member, job coach, or support worker. You do not have to handle this alone.
5
Report it to the FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov or call 1-877-382-4357.
💛
IT IS NOT YOUR FAULT
Being scammed is not a sign of weakness or stupidity. Scammers are professionals who fool millions of people every year. What matters is what you do next.
🔐 PASSWORDS GUIDE
PASSWORD SAFETY

Your passwords are the keys to your digital life. Here is how to keep them strong and safe.

💪
WHAT MAKES A STRONG PASSWORD
Long (12+ characters), random, with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. T#9kL!mP2@vQ is strong. password123 is not.
🚫
NEVER USE PERSONAL INFO
Birthdays, pet names, your child's name, your town - all of these are easy to guess. Avoid using anything that appears on your social media.
🔑
ONE PASSWORD PER ACCOUNT
Using the same password everywhere means one breach = all accounts compromised. A password manager makes this easy - it remembers all your passwords for you.
📱
TURN ON 2-FACTOR AUTH (2FA)
2FA sends a code to your phone when you log in. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot get in without your phone. Enable it on your email and bank apps first.
🤐
NEVER SHARE YOUR PASSWORDS
No legitimate company - your bank, Apple, Google, or a tech support agent - will ever ask for your password. If they do, it is a scam.
🌐 ONLINE SAFETY GUIDE
STAYING SAFE ONLINE

What you do online can affect your safety, privacy, and finances. These habits keep you protected.

🔒
LOOK FOR HTTPS
Before entering any personal info, check that the website address starts with https:// (with a padlock icon). The "s" means secure and encrypted.
📶
BE CAREFUL WITH PUBLIC WIFI
Coffee shops, airports, and libraries often have unsecured WiFi. Anyone on the same network could potentially see what you are doing. Never bank or log into sensitive accounts on public WiFi.
📲
ONLY DOWNLOAD FROM OFFICIAL STORES
The App Store (iPhone) and Google Play (Android) review apps for safety. Apps from random websites can contain malware. Stick to the official stores.
🤔
THINK BEFORE YOU POST
Once something is online, it is hard to take back. Avoid posting your home address, daily schedule, financial situation, or anything you would not want a stranger to know.
🚪
ALWAYS LOG OUT
On any shared device, always click "Sign Out" or "Log Out" when you are done. Just closing the browser window does NOT log you out.
💬 DIGITAL COMMUNICATION GUIDE
PROFESSIONAL DIGITAL COMMUNICATION

How you communicate digitally represents you. These habits make a good impression at work and in daily life.

📧
PROFESSIONAL EMAIL FORMAT
Do: Greet by name, use full sentences, check spelling, add a polite sign-off.
Do not: Use slang, ALL CAPS, text abbreviations (lol, omg), or forget a subject line.
⏱️
REPLY WITHIN ONE BUSINESS DAY
Responding within 24 hours during work days is the professional standard. Even a quick "Thank you, I will look into this" shows respect and reliability.
📢
ALL CAPS = SHOUTING
Writing in all capital letters is widely understood to mean you are angry or shouting. Even if you are just excited, it comes across poorly in professional settings.
👔
YOUR EMPLOYER CAN SEE YOUR SOCIAL MEDIA
Many employers check social media before hiring and after. Avoid posting about workplace conflicts, complaining about your job, or anything you would not say to your boss's face.
✅
READ BEFORE YOU SEND
Read every work email out loud before sending. This catches spelling errors, unintended tone, and missing information. You cannot un-send an email.
📱💻 YOUR DEVICES GUIDE
PHONES, TABLETS & COMPUTERS

These habits keep all your devices - phones, tablets, and computers - running well and your information safe.

🔄
KEEP ALL DEVICES UPDATED
Software updates fix security vulnerabilities on phones, tablets, AND computers. iPhone: Settings → General → Software Update. Android: Settings → System → Update. Windows: Settings → Update & Security. Mac: Apple menu → System Settings → Software Update.
☁️
BACK UP EVERYTHING
Phone/tablet: iCloud (iPhone) or Google Backup (Android) - turns on in Settings.
Computer: OneDrive or iCloud Drive for files. Windows Backup or Time Machine (Mac) for full backups.
A backup means a lost or broken device is an inconvenience, not a disaster.
🔐
LOCK EVERY DEVICE
Phones and tablets: use a PIN, fingerprint, or face unlock. Computers: set a login password and enable screen lock after inactivity (Settings → Lock Screen). Without a lock, anyone who picks up your device has instant access to your accounts and files.
📲
DOWNLOAD FROM OFFICIAL SOURCES ONLY
Phone/tablet: App Store (iPhone) or Google Play (Android) only.
Computer: Download programs only from the developer's official website or the Microsoft/Mac App Store. Random websites offering free software are a major source of viruses and malware.
🔁
RESTART FIXES MOST PROBLEMS
Device running slow? Apps crashing? Battery draining? Restart first - it works for phones AND computers. On a computer it also installs pending updates. Make it your first response to any technical problem.
🚫
NEVER PLUG IN UNKNOWN USB DRIVES
USB drives found in parking lots, lobbies, or sent unsolicited are a classic hacker technique. Plugging one into your computer can instantly install malware. This does not apply to phones (USB-C cables are fine), but any USB storage device from an unknown source is dangerous.
🔎
REVIEW PERMISSIONS
Phone: Settings → Privacy → review Camera, Microphone, Location for each app.
Computer: Browsers also ask for camera and microphone access - only allow for sites you trust like video call apps. A recipe website does not need your microphone.
📞 CALL SCRIPTS
WHAT TO SAY
Real scripts for real situations. Practice these so the words feel natural when you need them.
📵
HANGING UP ON A POSSIBLE SCAMMER
"I need to verify this is real. I will call you back using the number on your official website."
Then hang up. You do not owe them an explanation.
🏦
CALLING YOUR BANK AFTER A SCAM
"Hi, I think I may have given my information to a scammer. Can you check my account for unusual activity and help me secure it?"
🤝
TELLING A TRUSTED PERSON
"Something happened online and I am not sure if it was a scam. Can you help me figure out what to do next?"
📧
CALLING IN SICK BY EMAIL
Subject: Out Sick Today

Hi [Name],

I am not feeling well and will not be able to come in today. I will keep you updated on my recovery.

Thank you,
[Your Name]

❤️ THE STORY

TechReady was built as the seventh piece of the LifeReady suite. Digital life skills are some of the most important - and most overlooked - skills there are. Recognizing a scam, protecting your passwords, knowing what to post and what to keep private: these things affect your safety and independence every single day.

This app was built because digital literacy training does not always happen. Scammers specifically target people who haven't had it. TechReady is here to change that - free, available anytime, built with the same care as every other app in the suite.

🚀 THE MISSION

Digital safety should not be a privilege. TechReady helps people practice the real skills of digital life - spotting scams, protecting passwords, staying safe online, communicating professionally, and understanding their devices. Free, accessible, and built for everyone.

🤝 A DEDICATION

For the job coaches, support workers, and educators who know that digital independence is real independence. And for everyone who has ever been tricked online, felt confused by technology, or wondered if they could ever feel confident in the digital world. You can. You will.
