🎮 WHAT IS TRAVELREADY?

TravelReady is a free travel skills trainer. It helps you practice reading bus schedules, navigating routes, understanding fares, staying safe, and planning trips - at your own pace, with no pressure.

🕹️ THE GAME MODES
🎓
Practice Mode - No score, no pressure. Hints always available. Good for learning at your own pace.
🗺️
Transit Challenge - 10 mixed travel questions, scored, with streaks and levels. The main challenge mode.
🚌
Bus & Train - Focus on schedules, routes, stops, and transit knowledge.
🧭
Getting Around - Navigation, asking for help, crosswalks, and what to do if lost.
💰
Money & Travel - Fares, day passes, budgeting your trip, exact change.
📋
Planning a Trip - Schedules, timing, routes, and backup plans.
🛡️
Travel Safety - Staying safe, emergencies, trusting your instincts.
📅
Daily Challenge - 5 quick questions every day. Builds a daily streak.

🚌 TRANSIT GUIDE
🚌 READING A BUS SCHEDULE
1
Find your route number. Each bus line has a number (like Route 5 or Route 12). Look for signs at your stop or check the transit app.
2
Find your stop. Schedules list stops in order. Find the stop name or ID closest to where you are boarding.
3
Check the direction. Most routes go two ways - northbound/southbound or toward different destinations. Make sure you pick the right direction.
4
Find your time. Look across the row for your stop to find when buses arrive. Pick the time closest to when you need to travel.
5
Check the day. Schedules often have separate columns for weekdays vs weekends. Always use the right column for your travel day.
🔤 COMMON TRANSIT TERMS
📍
Stop / Station - Where you board or exit. Stops are for buses; stations are usually for trains or subway.
🔀
Transfer - Switching from one bus or train to another to continue your trip.
⚡
Express - Skips some stops to go faster. Good for longer trips but may not stop near you.
🔵
Local - Stops at every stop on the route. Slower but more flexible.
⏱️
Headway - How often a bus comes. A 15-minute headway means a bus every 15 minutes.
🔔
Request Stop - Press the button before your stop so the driver knows to stop there.
💡 TIPS FOR NEW RIDERS
💡
Arrive at your stop 5 minutes early. Buses sometimes run a little ahead of schedule.
💡
Have your exact fare or transit card ready before you board. Do not count money while people wait behind you.
💡
If unsure, ask the driver - "Does this bus go to [destination]?" They are used to helping riders.
💡
When in doubt, use the transit app. Most cities have a free official app with real-time arrivals.
🗺️ ROUTE MAP GUIDE
🗺️ READING A TRANSIT MAP
1
Find the legend first. Every map has a key that explains what colors, line styles, and symbols mean. Always read this before using the map.
2
Each color = one route. On most transit maps, each bus or train line has its own color. Follow the color to see where it goes.
3
Dots = stops. Small dots along a line show where the bus or train stops. Larger dots or circles often mark major hubs or transfer points.
4
Where lines cross = transfer point. If two colored lines cross, you can usually transfer between them at that stop or station.
5
Find your start and end. Locate where you are now and where you want to go. Trace the routes that connect them - that is your trip.
🧭 DIRECTIONS ON A MAP
N
North - Usually up on a map. Northbound means the vehicle is heading north (up).
S
South - Usually down on a map. Southbound = heading south (down).
E
East - Usually right on a map. Eastbound = heading east (right).
W
West - Usually left on a map. Westbound = heading west (left).
↔️
Inbound / Outbound - Some systems use inbound (toward downtown) and outbound (away from downtown) instead of compass directions.
🔀 PLANNING A ROUTE WITH TRANSFERS
1
Find your starting stop on the map.
2
Find your destination stop.
3
If no single line connects them, look for a transfer point - a stop where two routes meet.
4
Write down: Route 1 → Transfer stop → Route 2 → Destination.
5
Check the schedule for both routes so you know the transfer timing.
📅 SCHEDULES GUIDE
📅 HOW TO READ A SCHEDULE
📋
Columns = times. Each column in a schedule usually represents one trip. The times in that column show when the bus reaches each stop on that trip.
📋
Rows = stops. Each row shows a different stop on the route. Find your stop in the rows to see when the bus arrives there.
📋
Dashes (-) mean the bus does not stop there on that particular trip. Look for the next column with a time for your stop.
📋
Bold times often mark express trips or peak-hour service. Check the schedule legend to know for sure.
📋
AM and PM matter. Make sure you are reading the right half of the schedule - 9:15 AM and 9:15 PM are very different trips.
📆 WEEKDAY VS WEEKEND SERVICE
MON-FRI
Most routes run their most frequent service on weekdays, especially during morning and evening rush hours (7-9 AM and 4-7 PM).
SAT
Saturday service is usually reduced - fewer trips, longer waits. Check the Saturday column specifically.
SUN
Sunday service is often the most reduced. Some routes do not run at all on Sundays. Always check.
🎉
Holidays usually follow Sunday or reduced schedules. Check the transit agency website before holiday travel.
⏱️ PLANNING YOUR TIMING
💡
Add buffer time. If you need to be somewhere at 9:00 AM, aim for the bus that gets you there by 8:45 AM.
💡
Know the last bus. Always check when the last bus of the night runs so you are never stranded.
💡
Check for alerts. Before you travel, check the transit app for service delays, detours, or cancellations.
💰 FARES & PASSES
💰 TYPES OF FARES
🪙
Single Ride - You pay one fare each time you board. Good for occasional trips. Most buses require exact change or a card.
📅
Day Pass - A flat fee for unlimited rides in one day. Worth it if you are taking 3 or more trips.
📆
Weekly Pass - Covers 7 days of unlimited rides. Good for regular commuters.
📇
Monthly Pass - Best value if you ride every day. One upfront cost, unlimited rides for the whole month.
🔄
Transfer - Many systems allow a free or reduced transfer to another route within a time window (usually 90 minutes to 2 hours).
🪙 HOW TO PAY
💳
Transit Card - A reloadable card you tap or swipe. Faster than cash and often slightly cheaper per ride. Load money at a machine or online.
💵
Exact Cash - Many buses only accept exact change. Prepare your fare before you board.
📱
Mobile App - Some systems let you buy and show tickets on your phone. Check if your local transit has an app.
🎫
Paper Ticket - Purchased at a machine. Insert or show when boarding. Keep it until the end of your trip.
🎫 REDUCED FARES - DO YOU QUALIFY?
♿
Disability - People with disabilities often qualify for reduced fares. Ask at the transit office or check their website for the application process.
👴
Seniors - Most systems offer reduced fares for riders 65 and older. You may need to show ID.
🎓
Students - Many systems offer student discounts with a valid student ID.
👶
Children - Young children (usually under 5) often ride free. Check your local system for the age cutoff.
🛡️ SAFETY GUIDE
🛡️ STAYING SAFE WHILE TRAVELING
✅
Trust your instincts. If something feels wrong, it probably is. You do not need a reason to move to a different seat, leave a stop, or go into a public building.
✅
Stay aware. Keep your head up. Notice where you are, who is nearby, and what is happening around you. Avoid staring at your phone the whole trip.
✅
Keep valuables out of sight. Put your phone and wallet in a bag or pocket. Do not display them openly.
✅
Sit near the driver or other passengers if you feel uncomfortable. Avoid isolated seating at the back of an empty bus.
✅
Tell someone your plan. Let a trusted person know your route and expected arrival time when traveling somewhere new.
🚨 IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG
🚨
If you feel unsafe on a bus or train - Move toward the driver or other passengers. Tell the driver. Use the emergency button if needed.
🚨
If you are being followed - Walk into a busy store, restaurant, or transit station. Tell a worker. Call someone you trust.
🚨
If you are lost - Stop. Look for street signs. Find a public place with staff. Use your phone map or ask a transit worker.
🚨
If your phone dies - Find a library, transit station, or store. Ask to use a phone or ask staff for directions.
🚨
Real emergency - Call 911. Transit staff can also call for help. Emergency buttons on vehicles connect directly to transit control.
👤 WHO TO ASK FOR HELP
1
Bus or train driver - Always your first option. They know the routes and are trained to assist riders.
2
Transit station staff - Workers at stations can answer questions, help with fares, and call for assistance.
3
Store or business employees - A safe, reliable option when transit staff are not nearby.
4
Police or security - For safety concerns or real emergencies.
📞 CALL SCRIPTS
📞 Not sure what to say? These scripts give you the exact words for common travel situations. Read them, practice them, then make them your own.
🚌 ASKING IF A BUS GOES TO YOUR DESTINATION
WHEN BOARDING OR CALLING TRANSIT
"Excuse me - does this bus go to [destination]?"
If you are calling: "Hi, I need to get from [your location] to [destination]. Can you tell me which bus to take and where to catch it?"
📞 CALLING THE TRANSIT INFORMATION LINE
SCRIPT
"Hi, I need help planning a trip. I am trying to get from [address or area] to [address or area] on [day] around [time]. Can you tell me which bus or train to take?"
💡 Write down the route number, stop name, and departure time they give you. Read it back to confirm.
🆘 CALLING FOR HELP WHEN LOST
CALLING A TRUSTED PERSON
"Hi, it is me. I think I am lost. I am near [street sign / landmark / business name]. I was trying to get to [destination]. Can you help me figure out what to do?"
💡 Stay where you are while you wait for help or instructions. Do not keep walking if you are unsure where you are going.
♿ REQUESTING ACCESSIBILITY ASSISTANCE
TO A DRIVER OR STATION STAFF
"Excuse me, I need the ramp / a priority seat / a little extra time to board. Can you help me?"
You are always allowed to ask for these. Transit staff are trained to assist. You do not need to explain your disability.
🎫 ASKING ABOUT REDUCED FARE
AT THE TICKET OFFICE OR BY PHONE
"Hi, I have a disability and I wanted to ask about reduced fare programs. Can you tell me what is available and what I need to apply?"
💡 Most systems have reduced fare cards for people with disabilities. The savings can add up significantly over time - it is worth asking.
🚨 REPORTING A SAFETY CONCERN
TO THE DRIVER
"Excuse me, I wanted to let you know that [describe what you saw - e.g. there is an unattended bag / someone is bothering me / I feel unsafe near the person in the back]. Can you help?"
You never have to deal with safety issues alone on a bus or train. Tell the driver. That is part of their job.

🩵 THE STORY

TravelReady was built as the fifth piece of the LifeReady suite - a collection of free life skills trainers for everyone. After building tools for money, time, work, and home, it was clear that knowing how to get from place to place independently is just as essential as any of those skills.

Getting around on your own changes everything. It opens up jobs, friendships, appointments, and freedom. TravelReady exists to make that feel less intimidating and more possible.

🚀 THE MISSION

Independent travel should not be a mystery. Reading a bus schedule, planning a route, knowing what to do if you get lost - these skills can be learned. TravelReady makes that learning free, accessible, and built around the way real people actually travel.

🤝 A DEDICATION

For the job coaches, life skills instructors, support workers, and teachers who spend their days helping people take the next step. And for every person who has ever stood at a bus stop feeling unsure - this one is for you.
